2. What data does Calarm process?

Calarm accesses your Google Calendar in read-only mode. The following data is processed:

  • Google OAuth token: Stored locally on your device (iOS Keychain) to access the Google Calendar API.
  • Calendar and event data: Retrieved from the Google Calendar API and stored locally on your device to schedule alarms.
  • Settings: Your alarm settings (lead time, excluded calendars/events, pause mode) are stored locally on your device.

3. Where is data stored?

Exclusively on your device. Calarm does not operate any servers. No data is transmitted to us or third parties — except for direct communication between your device and the Google Calendar API.

4. Third-party sharing

Calarm does not share any data with third parties. The only external communication is with:

  • Google Calendar API: To retrieve your calendars and events (read-only). Google's Privacy Policy applies.
  • Google Sign-In: To authenticate your Google account.

5. How long is data stored?

Your data is stored on your device as long as you use the app. When you sign out or delete the app, all local data is removed.

6. Your rights (GDPR)

You have the right to:

  • Access information about your stored data
  • Delete your data (by signing out or uninstalling the app)
  • Revoke Google access (via your Google account at myaccount.google.com/permissions)

7. Analytics and tracking

Calarm does not use any analytics tools, trackers, or advertising. No usage data is collected.
